HAYSTACK HAVEN

Cannon Beach

Nestled in the heart of Cannon Beach, Oregon. Haystack Haven is a four-acre mature forest, overlooking the iconic Haystack Rock and serves as a new model to reimagine the relationship between development and ecological stewardship.

LIVING DATA POD

San Francisco, CA

The Living Data Pod is an autonomous floating platform designed to provide agency to local communities to test water quality and monitor ecological health. A central bio-material enclosure houses a "Planktonscope", an open source imaging platform for citizen oceanography adapted to transmit visual data to local coastal communities.

OCEAN IMAGINEER

Hong Kong

The Ocean Imagineer is a floating oyster hatchery and solar hydrogen production plant pilot project. We are building a floating oyster hatchery and setting it up in North point. On the roof, solar panels will power the production of hydrogen, the cleanest fuel for the future.

NUXALK RISING

Bella Coola, B.C.

ABOUT

Regenerative Design: The Future of Sustainable Living

Posted on May 15, 2024 by Jane Doe

In this post, we explore the principles of regenerative design and how they can be applied to create more sustainable and resilient communities. Regenerative design goes beyond sustainability by actively improving the environment and ecosystems it interacts with.

Key principles of regenerative design include:

  • Whole systems thinking
  • Integrating with local ecosystems
  • Promoting biodiversity
  • Enhancing human and environmental health

By implementing these principles, we can create buildings and communities that not only reduce their environmental impact but actively contribute to the health of the planet. This approach is crucial for addressing climate change and ensuring a sustainable future for generations to come.

The Power of Biodesign in Urban Environments

Posted on April 22, 2024 by John Smith

Discover how biodesign is revolutionizing urban planning and architecture, creating cities that work in harmony with nature. Biodesign integrates living organisms and biological processes into the built environment, offering innovative solutions to urban challenges.

Some exciting applications of biodesign in urban environments include:

  • Living buildings with algae facades for air purification
  • Mycoremediation for soil and water cleanup
  • Biomimetic materials inspired by nature's efficiency
  • Urban food forests and vertical gardens

These innovations not only improve the ecological footprint of our cities but also enhance the quality of life for urban dwellers. As we face increasing urbanization and environmental pressures, biodesign offers a path towards more resilient and sustainable urban futures.

Community-Driven Conservation: A Case Study

Posted on March 10, 2024 by Emily Chen

Learn about a successful community-led conservation project that's making a real difference in protecting local ecosystems. This case study demonstrates the power of grassroots initiatives in environmental stewardship.

The project, located in coastal Oregon, focuses on restoring and protecting a critical estuary habitat. Key elements of the project include:

  • Community engagement and education programs
  • Collaborative partnerships with local indigenous groups
  • Innovative funding models combining public and private resources
  • Adaptive management strategies based on ongoing scientific monitoring

This case study highlights how community-driven initiatives can achieve significant conservation outcomes while also fostering social cohesion and economic benefits. It serves as an inspiring model for other communities looking to take an active role in protecting their local environments.
